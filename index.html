Introduction & The Challenge

The Problem

Wearable devices continuously collect valuable health data (sleep patterns, activity levels) that critically affect health, yet this data remains underused in clinical tools and standard LLMs struggle to contextualize raw sensor metrics.

Current Limitations

  • Underutilized wearable data
  • Lack of personalized insights
  • Generic health recommendations
  • Poor sensor data interpretation

Our Opportunity

  • 24/7 continuous monitoring
  • Personal pattern recognition
  • Tailored health coaching
  • AI-powered interpretation

Model Architecture

Base Model: Gemini Ultra 1.0

Two-stage fine-tuning process to create PH-LLM

Stage 1
Text-Based Fine-tuning

Fine-tune full Gemini model on case-study Q&A and coding tasks to interpret sensor data

Stage 2
Multimodal Adapter

Train MLP adapter that encodes daily sensor summaries into LLM's latent space

Architecture Benefits

  • Preserves base model capabilities while adding health expertise
  • Efficient adapter training vs. full model retraining
  • Seamless integration of multimodal sensor data
  • Scalable to additional health domains

Figure 1: Study Design & Performance Overview

📋 Part A: Experimental Design

Schematic showing the overall experiment design of the study. PH-LLM was evaluated on three aspects of personal health: (1) assessing its level of expert knowledge based on certification examination-style MCQs; (2) generating personalized insights and recommendations for user goals in the sleep and fitness domains; and (3) predicting PROs for sleep quality from aggregated daily-resolution numerical sensor data.

📊 Part B: Performance Results

Performance of PH-LLM, as contextualized with the responses of human experts, for professional examinations, coaching recommendations and PROs for sleep quality. Data are presented as mean accuracy (n = 629 sleep questions and n = 99 fitness examination questions), mean human expert rating (n = 4,265 sleep and n = 5,049 fitness case study ratings across principles and subsections from PH-LLM and n = 2,606 and n = 3,335 from human experts) and AUROC (n = 833 survey responses) bootstrapped over 1,000, 1,000 and 100 iterations, respectively.

Statistical Significance

  • Error bars represent 95% confidence intervals
  • '*' indicates statistically significant difference between groups
  • Two-sided t-test for examinations (P = 1.52 × 10⁻¹⁰ for fitness)
  • Two-sided Wilcoxon rank-sum test for ratings (P = 3.31 × 10⁻¹¹ for sleep)
  • 'Naive performance' achieved by random classifier
  • HR: heart rate; HRV: heart rate variability

Limitations & Considerations

⚠️ Current Limitations

  • Subjective evaluation bias in human ratings
  • Limited demographic representation
  • Daily aggregates vs. raw time-series data
  • Occasional model confabulations
  • Single-domain focus (sleep & fitness)

🔬 Important Considerations

  • Not intended to replace medical professionals
  • Requires careful safety validation
  • Need for diverse training populations
  • Ethical considerations for health AI
  • Privacy and data security requirements

🛡️ Safety & Ethics

All health AI systems require rigorous testing for safety, bias, and clinical appropriateness before deployment. PH-LLM represents a research prototype demonstrating feasibility, not a clinical tool.

Technical Limitations

  • Sensor data quality depends on device accuracy and user compliance
  • Model performance varies with individual baseline health status
  • Limited ability to capture complex medical interactions
